theGivingTree

make your donations go further

From finding where to donate, to which charities are most in need, giving is filled with pitfalls for the average donor. theGivingTree aims to help donors donate easily and effectively to the causes they care about while providing a way for donors to track their donations over time.
This project was worked on by two different teams for two separate classes: Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction followed by User Interface Design Project. The information below will predominantly focus on work during User Interface Design Project where I contributed as a UX researcher and designer. 

medium-fi prototype

Through iterations of testing, heuristic evaluations, and implementing changes, the medium-fidelity prototype (with its tasks outlined below) was developed as the final project of the course Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction. This version was then picked up in User Interface Design Project for further iteration towards a high-fidelity prototype. 


simple

Donate to a cause effectively without having to do research on organizations

moderate

Set up recurring donations for different organizations through causes you care about

complex

See the organizations/amounts you’ve donated to long-term and retrieve your tax-forms

usability study

After implementing Firebase Realtime Database as the backend of theGivingTree to the current state of the medium-fi prototype, we conducted usability testing on 5 frequent donors using the following procedure.  

testing insights

Background Questions

What is your relationship to donating? 
Do you have organizations or causes you donate to? If not, is there a reason why you do not donate to organizations? 
How do you find the organizations you donate to?

Probing 
Questions

What were some parts of the app that were difficult to follow? Useful? 
Would you actually use this app? 
What would make you more likely to make a recurring donation? 

participant attempts tasks

Comfort produce husband boy her had hearing. Law others theirs passed but wishes. You day real less till dear read. Considered use dispatched melancholy sympathize discretion led. 

testing metrics 

Engagement: Number of clicks 
Efficiency: Time to complete task 
Robustness: Number of errors made when completing a task
